Inclusive When our differences are embraced and respected help us develop better solutions that benefit everyone, rather than some. Guiding principles

Individual Rights We are each created with equal individual rights to control and to defend our life, liberty and property and to voluntary contractual exchange.

A lean government Governments main objective ought to be to secure individual rights. Moreover, government ought to nurture and encourage its citizens through incentives, rather than disincentives in the form of taxes and bureaucratic red tape.

Equal justice Government must treat everyone equally; neither rewarding failure nor punishing success.

Subsidiarity Government authority must reside at the lowest feasible level.

Spontaneous Order When individual rights are respected, unregulated competition will maximize economic benefit for society by providing the most goods and services possible at the lowest cost.

Freedom Freedom of thought, worship, speech, and association are all essential to create an innovative, inclusive, and effective population.

The Golden Rule Deal with others honestly and require honesty in return.
